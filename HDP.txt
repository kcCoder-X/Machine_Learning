FBS refers to Fasting Blood Sugar
Fasting blood sugar (FBS) measures glucose in your blood after an overnight fast (8-12 hours) to check for diabetes or prediabetes, with normal levels below 100 mg/dL; 100-125 mg/dL suggests prediabetes; and 126 mg/dL or higher indicates diabetes, helping assess how your body manages sugar and identifying risks like heart or nerve damage
Elevated fasting blood sugar is a risk factor for cardiovascular disease because:
It indicates impaired glucose metabolism or diabetes
Diabetes significantly increases the risk of heart disease and heart attacks
High blood sugar can damage blood vessels and nerves that control the heart

Typical BP ranges in analysis:
Low risk: <120 mmHg
Moderate: 120-140 mmHg
High risk: >140 mmHg

EKG (Electrocardiogram) Results in Heart Disease
An EKG/ECG (Electrocardiogram) records the electrical activity of your heart and can reveal abnormalities that indicate heart disease or increased risk.
Common EKG Categories in Heart Disease Datasets
Most heart disease datasets classify resting EKG results into 3 categories:
0 = Normal
No abnormalities detected
Regular rhythm and normal wave patterns

1 = ST-T Wave Abnormality
Changes in the ST segment or T wave
Can indicate:
Ischemia (reduced blood flow to heart)
Previous heart attack
Electrolyte imbalances
Medication effects
Ranges from mild to concerning

2 = Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)
Thickening of the heart's left ventricle wall
Usually caused by:
High blood pressure (most common)
Heart valve disease
Cardiomyopathy
Indicates the heart is working harder than normal
Strong predictor of heart disease and complications

maximum heart rate (MHR) is the highest number of beats per minute (bpm) your heart can achieve during intense exertion, often estimated by subtracting your age from 220 (e.g., 220 - age), but more accurate formulas like 211 - (0.64 x age) exist, with real testing being the best method, all used to set target heart rate zones for exercise. 

Exercise angina refers to chest pain or discomfort that occurs during physical activity and is a key symptom of coronary artery disease.
In Heart Disease Datasets
Exercise angina is typically coded as binary:
1 (Yes): Patient experiences chest pain during exercise
0 (No): No chest pain during exercise

ST depression refers to a specific abnormality seen on an EKG (electrocardiogram) that indicates the heart muscle is not getting enough oxygen (ischemia). It's a critical marker for coronary artery disease.
Severity levels:
Mild: 0.5-1.0 mm depression
Moderate: 1.0-2.0 mm depression
Severe: >2.0 mm depression

The ST slope refers to the angle or direction of the ST segment on an EKG during exercise stress testing as it moves from the S wave toward the T wave:
Measured during peak exercise or recovery
Describes the geometric pattern of ST changes
Helps distinguish between normal and abnormal responses

In Heart Disease Datasets
Typical Encoding:
1 = Upsloping (best prognosis)
2 = Flat (intermediate)
3 = Downsloping (worst prognosis)

Ischemia is a serious condition where blood flow and oxygen are restricted to a part of the body.
CAD, or Coronary Artery Disease, is the most common type of heart disease where plaque (cholesterol, fats) builds up in the heart's arteries (atherosclerosis), narrowing them and restricting blood/oxygen flow, causing chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, or heart attack, and is managed with lifestyle changes, meds, and procedures.

Thallium (specifically Thallium-201) is primarily related to heart disease through its use in the Thallium Stress Test, a nuclear imaging test that shows how well blood flows to your heart muscle during rest and exercise, helping diagnose Coronary Artery Disease (CAD), identify blockages, assess damage, and predict cardiac risk before surgery or after a heart attack.
Procedure:
Radioactive thallium-201 injected into bloodstream
Patient exercises (or given medication to simulate exercise)
Thallium travels through blood to heart muscle
Special camera (gamma camera) takes pictures
Shows which areas of heart receive adequate blood flow
